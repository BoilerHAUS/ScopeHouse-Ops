# ScopeHouse Design Partner Qualification Rubric v1

## Purpose

Define who should be accepted into the early design partner group.

This rubric is meant to help with:

- outreach triage
- post-call qualification
- beta onboarding decisions
- paid pilot and service offer targeting

It should be used with `docs/customer-interview-script.md`.

## Core principle

A design partner is not any interested person.

A design partner should have:

- a real renovation control problem
- a live or near-live project context
- enough complexity for ScopeHouse to matter
- enough urgency to engage now
- enough collaboration to provide useful feedback
- enough openness to work through an early product or founder-led setup

## Qualification method

Use two layers:

1. Hard gate check
2. Scored rubric

A lead should pass the hard gate, then score well enough on the rubric to justify onboarding.

## 1. Hard gate check

A lead should usually pass all of these.

### Hard gate questions

- Is the project active now, or starting soon?
- Is the renovation large enough or complex enough that mistakes are costly?
- Does the person have direct control over project decisions, or strong influence over them?
- Is the pain real today, not only theoretical?
- Is the person open to a follow-up, beta, or design partner conversation?

### Hard gate result

If the answer is no on two or more of the above, do not treat as a design partner.

Possible label:

- not-now
- too-early
- too-small
- no-decision-authority
- low-urgency

## 2. Scored rubric

Score each category from 0 to 3.

### A. Project complexity

0
Very small project. Single-room cosmetic work. Little coordination needed.

1
Some planning complexity, but limited phases, budget pressure, or coordination.

2
Multi-room or multi-phase work with moderate risk and several moving parts.

3
Medium to large renovation with clear coordination burden, multiple stakeholders, or meaningful financial risk.

### B. Problem intensity

0
Problem sounds vague or polite. No concrete examples.

1
Some frustration exists, but impact is limited.

2
Clear pain with specific examples around scope, quotes, budget, decisions, or changes.

3
Strong pain with visible cost, delay, rework, dispute, or repeated confusion.

### C. Timing and urgency

0
No active timeline. Problem is distant.

1
Project exists, but urgency is weak.

2
Project is in planning, quote collection, pre-construction, or active job stage now.

3
Problem is immediate. They need a better system soon to avoid cost or confusion.

### D. Decision authority

0
No real authority or influence.

1
Some influence, but another person controls the decision.

2
Strong influence over process or tool choice.

3
Direct decision maker or budget owner.

### E. Workflow fit

0
Needs a very different category of tool, such as full ERP, accounting, or procurement.

1
Some overlap, but workflow fit is weak.

2
Good fit for planning, quote comparison, documentation, decision tracking, or change control.

3
Excellent fit. Their problem maps directly to ScopeHouse core workflows.

### F. Collaboration value

0
Likely to provide little useful feedback.

1
May provide occasional reaction, but weak detail.

2
Can explain workflow clearly and provide grounded examples.

3
Thoughtful, responsive, and willing to share examples, documents, or repeated feedback.

### G. Payment signal

0
No spend, no urgency, no budget path.

1
Weak or unclear willingness to pay.

2
Some spend already exists, or a reasonable path to pilot or service payment exists.

3
Strong signal. They already spend to patch the problem, or show credible openness to paying for service or early product support.

## Score interpretation

### 17 to 21
Strong design partner candidate.

Action:
- prioritize follow-up
- invite to design partner group
- consider founder-led onboarding
- consider paid pilot or service conversation if pain is active

### 12 to 16
Possible design partner.

Action:
- keep warm
- follow up if timing improves
- use for interviews, message testing, or future beta

### 7 to 11
Useful research lead, but weak design partner fit.

Action:
- keep in research pool
- do not spend high-touch onboarding effort yet

### 0 to 6
Do not onboard as design partner.

Action:
- log reason
- revisit only if project status changes

## Exclusion criteria

Do not prioritize as design partner if any of these are true:

- project is too small to justify structured control
- project is too far in the future
- person is not close enough to the real workflow
- they want a full contractor operating suite, accounting system, or procurement stack
- they only give generic positive feedback with no concrete pain
- they show no openness to follow-up, testing, or founder interaction
- they are looking mainly for lead generation or marketplace value

## Segment guidance

### Homeowners

Strong homeowner design partner if:

- the renovation is meaningful in cost or scope
- multiple decisions are in motion
- quotes are still being collected or compared
- family or contractor coordination is already messy
- they feel under-organized before work is fully locked

### Solo GCs and consultants

Strong pro design partner if:

- they run several moving parts themselves
- client communication and decision tracking matter directly to them
- they do not want heavyweight contractor software
- they can describe where current tools break down
- they see client-facing value in cleaner project control

## Recommended labels after scoring

Use one primary label.

- design-partner-priority
- design-partner-possible
- research-only
- paid-service-candidate
- paid-pilot-candidate
- not-now

You can add one reason tag.

- too-small
- too-early
- weak-pain
- weak-authority
- weak-fit
- no-budget-signal

## Next action rules

### If strong design partner candidate

- schedule follow-up within 7 days
- invite to early onboarding or design partner track
- ask for deeper workflow detail or sample artifacts if useful

### If possible design partner

- keep warm with updates
- revisit when project timing tightens
- use for message and workflow testing

### If research-only

- capture the insight
- do not use high-touch onboarding time

### If paid-service-candidate

- route toward founder-led setup, quote comparison, or planning support

## Interviewer reminder

The goal is not to collect the nicest people.

The goal is to find users with:

- real pain
- real timing
- real complexity
- real fit
- real feedback value
- real payment potential
