# ScopeHouse Discovery Call Script v1

## Purpose

Create a short founder-led discovery call script for homeowner and pro leads.

This is a sales call structure, not a research interview.

The goal is to:

- confirm fit
- surface active pain
- decide whether to route toward design partner, founder-led service, paid pilot, or no next step

## Call length

Target:

15 to 20 minutes

## Core rules

1. Keep the call conversational.
2. Do not over-explain the product too early.
3. Ask for current pain and current workflow.
4. Confirm whether there is a live project and a real decision maker.
5. End every call with a specific next step or a clear no.

## Best use cases

Use this script for:

- warm intros
- waitlist leads
- referrals
- design partner screening
- service offer screening

## Script

### 1. Opener

Use:

Thanks for taking the time. I wanted to learn a bit about your renovation or project process, where things feel messy right now, and whether ScopeHouse might be a fit. I’ll keep this tight.

### 2. Context questions

Ask:

- What project are you working on right now?
- What stage is it in?
- Who is involved in the project?

What you need to learn:

- live project or not
- homeowner or pro
- project stage
- stakeholder count

### 3. Pain questions

Ask:

- What part of the project feels least under control right now?
- Where do scope, quotes, budget, decisions, or changes get messy?
- What has that confusion cost you so far?

What you need to learn:

- active pain
- real consequences
- front-door use case

### 4. Workflow questions

Ask:

- How are you keeping track of everything right now?
- Where do the notes, quotes, and decisions live?
- What gets lost or becomes hard to compare?

What you need to learn:

- current tools
- scattered record problem
- whether ScopeHouse fits the actual workflow

### 5. Fit check

Use:

What ScopeHouse does best is help bring renovation planning and control into one clear record, especially around scope, budget, quotes, decisions, and changes.

Then ask:

- Does that line up with the kind of problem you are dealing with?
- Which part of that feels most useful?
- What would you need to see for this to feel worth trying?

What you need to learn:

- resonance
- strongest value hook
- objections or missing trust

### 6. Route question

If pain is high and project is live, ask:

- Would it be more useful for you to test the software directly, or to have help setting the project up with you?

For pros, add:

- Would this fit better as an ongoing tool for your projects, or something we use together on a current client job first?

What you need to learn:

- design partner fit
- service fit
- paid pilot fit

### 7. Close

Use one of these:

#### If strong design partner fit

You look like a strong fit for our early design partner group. The next step would be a deeper workflow session where we look at your project in more detail.

#### If strong service fit

I think the fastest path here might be a founder-led setup where we organize the project with you and turn it into a cleaner working record.

#### If weak fit

I do not want to force this where it does not fit. It sounds like the timing or workflow may not be right yet, but I can keep you posted as the product evolves.

## Note-taking fields

Capture:

- segment
- project stage
- top pain point
- current tools
- workflow fit
- urgency
- decision authority
- likely route
- next action

## Route outcomes

Use one:

- design partner follow-up
- founder-led service follow-up
- paid pilot follow-up
- keep warm
- no fit

## Objections to watch for

### We already use spreadsheets

Response:

That is common. The question is whether those records are still clean enough when quotes, decisions, and changes start moving fast.

### I do not want heavy software

Response:

That is exactly what ScopeHouse is trying to avoid. The goal is a cleaner control layer, not a full contractor system.

### We are too early

Response:

That may be true, but early planning is also where weak records start becoming expensive later.

## Reminder

This call is not about closing with pressure.

It is about identifying:

- real pain
- live timing
- workflow fit
- best next step
