# ScopeHouse Positioning v1

## Purpose

Lock the first external positioning for ScopeHouse.

This document is for homepage messaging, outreach, demos, design partner conversations, and early sales work.

## One-line positioning

ScopeHouse is a renovation planning and control platform for homeowners and small renovation pros who need one clear record for scope, budget, schedule, decisions, and changes.

## Short paragraph version

ScopeHouse helps homeowners, solo general contractors, renovation consultants, and small renovation firms turn messy renovation projects into structured plans and controlled execution. It brings project intake, scope, budget, schedule, documents, decisions, and change tracking into one shared system. The first wedge is homeowners planning medium to large renovations, then solo pros who need stronger project control without heavyweight contractor software.

## Primary early wedge

### Core wedge now

Homeowners planning medium to large renovations.

Best fit signals:

- more than one room, phase, or contractor involved
- meaningful budget pressure
- uncertainty before quotes are collected
- a need to compare bids and decisions clearly
- a project large enough that mistakes are expensive

### Expansion wedge next

Solo general contractors, renovation consultants, and design-build operators who need stronger planning and control without enterprise overhead.

## Problem ScopeHouse solves

ScopeHouse exists for renovation projects where the work is not yet under control.

Users usually have:

- unclear scope
- weak budget clarity
- quote comparison confusion
- schedule drift risk
- scattered files and photos
- missing decision records
- poor visibility into changes

## What makes ScopeHouse different

1. It starts before the job is fully priced or underway.
2. It focuses on planning and control, not back-office accounting.
3. It creates one shared project record across scope, budget, schedule, documents, decisions, and changes.
4. It gives users tangible outputs they can review and share.
5. It uses AI to support judgment, not replace it.

## What ScopeHouse is not

ScopeHouse is not:

- generic project management software
- full contractor ERP
- accounting software
- a lead generation marketplace
- a procurement system
- an AI autopilot that edits records without review
- a mobile-first field operations app

## Buyer outcome

The user starts with uncertainty and ends with control.

More specifically, ScopeHouse helps the user:

- define the job clearly
- organize the work before money is committed
- compare quotes against a real scope
- keep track of decisions and changes
- produce a credible project record to share with others

## First revenue motion

The first revenue motion should match the positioning.

Start with:

1. founder-led renovation setup and scope cleanup
2. paid quote comparison and planning support
3. paid pilots for solo pros

This works because the positioning is strongest where users need clarity before and during early project control, not after they already have a full contractor back-office stack.

## External use guidance

Use this positioning when writing:

- homepage copy
- waitlist copy
- outreach messages
- design partner invitations
- demo talk tracks
- investor or partner summaries

Do not widen the message into general construction software.
Do not frame ScopeHouse as an AI-first product.
Do not lead with features before the control problem is clear.

## Working message hierarchy

Lead with:

- renovation planning and control
- one clear project record
- scope, budget, schedule, decisions, and changes

Support with:

- quote comparison clarity
- cleaner homeowner and pro communication
- credible exports and summaries
- pro-grade structure without heavy software

## Open questions

1. Does the strongest paid demand come from homeowner-led planning or pro-led control?
2. Which phrase converts better, renovation planning and control, or renovation operating system?
3. Is quote comparison strong enough to be a front-door use case?
4. How much of the message should lean into homeowner and contractor collaboration versus solo project control?
