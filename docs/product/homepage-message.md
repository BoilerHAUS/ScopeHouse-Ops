# ScopeHouse Homepage Message Draft v1

## Purpose

Create the first homepage message set for ScopeHouse.

This draft is for:

- landing page copy
- waitlist page copy
- early demo page copy
- first public messaging tests

It follows the positioning in `docs/product/positioning.md`.

## Recommended homepage direction

Lead with the control problem.

Do not lead with AI.
Do not lead with generic project management language.
Do not sound like contractor ERP software.

The homepage should make one promise clear:

ScopeHouse helps users bring renovation work under control before and during execution.

## Hero copy

### Primary headline

Plan your renovation with less guesswork and more control.

### Primary subhead

ScopeHouse gives homeowners and small renovation pros one clear place to define scope, organize budget and schedule, compare quotes, track decisions, and manage changes.

### Primary CTA

Join the waitlist

### Secondary CTA

See how it works

## Alternate headline options

### Option B

### Option C

## Above-the-fold support points

Use 3 short support points near the hero.

- Define the job before costly mistakes stack up.
- Compare quotes against a real scope.
- Keep decisions and changes from getting lost.

## Value section

### Section headline

Bring the whole renovation into one working system.

### Section intro

Most renovation problems start before the work is under control. ScopeHouse helps you organize the project early, then keep the record clean as the job moves forward.

### Core value bullets

- Guided intake that turns rough ideas into a structured project plan.
- Scope, budget, and schedule organized in one shared record.
- Cleaner quote comparison with less guesswork and fewer omissions.
- Decision and change tracking that keeps the project auditable.
- Exportable summaries you can review, share, and act on.

## Problem section

### Section headline

Renovations break down when the record is scattered.

### Body copy

A large renovation usually starts with loose notes, rough budgets, unclear scope, and competing opinions. Then quotes come in, changes happen, and key decisions disappear into texts, emails, and memory. ScopeHouse gives the project one place to live before confusion turns into cost.

## How it works section

### Section headline

From rough idea to controlled project record.

### Step 1

Start with guided intake.
Capture the rooms, goals, priorities, budget range, timing, and constraints.

### Step 2

Build a working scope.
Turn intake into a structured scope you can review, edit, and share.

### Step 3

Bring budget and schedule into view.
Track the financial frame, project phases, and key milestones.

### Step 4

Run the job with a cleaner record.
Keep documents, photos, decisions, and changes tied to the same project.

## Segment proof points

Use these blocks or cards lower on the page.

### For homeowners

For homeowners planning a serious renovation.

- Get your project out of notebooks, texts, and half-finished spreadsheets.
- Build a clearer scope before you commit to quotes.
- Keep budget, decisions, and changes in one place.
- Share a more credible project summary with contractors and family stakeholders.

### For solo GCs and renovation pros

For small renovation pros who need stronger control without heavyweight software.

- Capture project details faster.
- Keep scope, documents, and changes tied together.
- Compare quotes and project records against real project intent.
- Give clients a clearer, more disciplined project experience.

## Trust section

### Section headline

Built for real renovation work.

### Body copy

ScopeHouse is designed for medium to large residential projects where mistakes are expensive and clarity matters. It supports the planning and control layer most tools skip, especially early in the job when scope, price, and decisions are still moving.

## AI message guidance

If AI is mentioned on the page, keep it in a support role.

Recommended copy:

AI helps draft scope, compare quotes, and summarize project activity. You stay in control of the record.

Do not place AI in the hero.
Do not frame ScopeHouse as an AI product first.

## Notes for design and messaging

- keep the tone calm and credible
- use plain language
- show structure, not hype
- avoid stock construction-software language
- avoid feature overload above the fold
- keep the first screen focused on control and clarity

## Message risks to avoid

Do not say:

- all-in-one construction software
- end-to-end contractor management suite
- AI-powered renovation platform
- built for everyone in construction

Those messages weaken the wedge and push ScopeHouse toward the wrong category.
