# ScopeHouse Launch Readiness Checklist v1

## Purpose

Define what must be true before ScopeHouse moves into private beta and before it moves into broader launch.

This document separates:

- private beta readiness
- broader launch readiness

They are not the same thing.

## Core rule

Do not use broader launch standards for private beta.
Do not use private beta standards as an excuse for broader launch.

## Launch stages

### Stage 1

Private beta

Meaning:

A controlled rollout to a small group of qualified users where the goal is learning, onboarding discipline, activation, and early revenue signal.

### Stage 2

Broader launch

Meaning:

A public or semi-public push where the goal is repeatable onboarding, clearer conversion, stronger trust, and a more stable operating rhythm.

## Private beta readiness checklist

### Messaging readiness

- positioning is documented
- homepage message exists
- discovery call script exists
- demo script exists
- objection handling sheet exists
- the team can explain ScopeHouse in one clear sentence

Private beta gate:

Message is stable enough for live calls, even if it is still being refined.

### Onboarding readiness

- beta onboarding checklist exists
- one founder can run onboarding repeatably
- the first success moment is defined
- follow-up checkpoints are defined
- post-session logging is defined

Private beta gate:

A new beta user can be onboarded without inventing the process from scratch.

### Support readiness

- one owner is responsible for user follow-up
- bug or friction capture exists
- design partner tracker is active
- next action is logged for every active beta user

Private beta gate:

No active beta user should be left without a clear next step.

### Pricing readiness

- pricing hypotheses are documented
- founder-led service offer exists
- likely route is clear, beta, service, or pilot
- at least one pricing conversation has been run against real users

Private beta gate:

The team is not guessing blindly about how to route strong leads.

### Metrics readiness

- weekly business metrics review exists
- minimum dashboard is defined
- activation metrics are identified
- design partner activity can be tracked weekly

Private beta gate:

The team can tell whether beta is producing learning or only activity.

### Product readiness

- project creation works
- intake can be completed
- scope draft or equivalent first structure can be reached
- at least one meaningful export or summary exists
- the core planning story can be shown in a demo

Private beta gate:

The user can reach the core planning loop and leave with a visible project record.

### Operational readiness

- design partner rubric exists
- design partner tracker exists
- interview and sales materials exist
- founder has capacity to onboard and follow up manually

Private beta gate:

The founder can support the first wave without losing track of who needs what.

## Private beta decision gate

You are ready for private beta if:

- the message is clear enough to sell in conversation
- onboarding is repeatable
- follow-up is controlled
- the core loop works
- metrics are being reviewed weekly
- qualified users are already in the pipeline

## Broader launch readiness checklist

### Messaging readiness

- positioning has been tested against real calls
- homepage message has been refined with feedback
- strongest value hook is known by segment
- core objections are documented and repeatable
- launch copy exists for website, outreach, and announcements

Broader launch gate:

Messaging is not only internally clear. It has real market feedback behind it.

### Onboarding readiness

- the beta onboarding process has been refined from live use
- common setup friction is known
- first session can be run efficiently
- the path from first contact to active use is documented

Broader launch gate:

New users can be onboarded with less founder improvisation.

### Support readiness

- support expectations are documented
- common questions are known
- escalation path for major friction exists
- active users are not piling up without response

Broader launch gate:

The team can absorb more users without support quality collapsing.

### Pricing readiness

- pricing has been pressure-tested in real conversations
- strongest route is clearer, service, pilot, or software
- strongest segment is clearer
- price objections are understood
- public-facing pricing or pricing logic is stable enough to show externally

Broader launch gate:

Pricing is not final, but it is grounded in real buyer behavior.

### Metrics readiness

- weekly review is operating consistently
- activation benchmark is known
- early retention benchmark is known
- lead-to-call and call-to-next-step conversion is understood
- service or pilot close rate is visible

Broader launch gate:

The business can tell whether launch is improving or degrading the funnel.

### Product readiness

- core loop is stable enough for more users
- repeated onboarding reveals the same main friction points, not chaos
- exports or outputs feel credible
- the product story holds up across more than one project type

Broader launch gate:

The product is strong enough that more traffic will produce learning, not only noise.

### Commercial readiness

- discovery process is repeatable
- demo process is repeatable
- route decisions are clear, design partner, service, pilot, product
- at least one revenue path is working better than the others

Broader launch gate:

There is a real path from interest to money, not only interest to more conversations.

## Broader launch decision gate

You are ready for broader launch if:

- private beta produced usable learning
- onboarding is tighter than manual improvisation
- pricing has real signal behind it
- the strongest segment is becoming clearer
- at least one route to revenue is working
- support and follow-up can handle more volume

## What should block launch

Do not move into private beta if:

- onboarding is still improvised every time
- the core loop is not reachable
- the team cannot follow up with active users
- there are no qualified users ready to test

Do not move into broader launch if:

- private beta is producing noise instead of learning
- messaging is still unstable in every conversation
- onboarding drops too many users after the first session
- there is still no credible route to money
- support load would break the workflow immediately

## Suggested review format

Before changing stages, answer:

- what is true today
- what is still missing
- what is the biggest launch risk
- what metric or workflow would most reduce that risk

## Reminder

The point of launch readiness is not perfection.

The point is to know:

- what stage you are entering
- what that stage is supposed to prove
- what must be true before taking the next step
