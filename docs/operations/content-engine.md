# ScopeHouse Content Engine Outline v1

## Purpose

Define a small, repeatable content system for ScopeHouse.

This content engine should support:

- trust
- discovery
- inbound interest
- design partner recruiting
- founder sales conversations
- sharper positioning over time

It should not turn into generic home improvement content.

## Core principle

Content should help the right buyer recognize their control problem.

It should not chase broad traffic.

## Target readers

### Primary reader now

Homeowners planning medium to large renovations.

They usually care about:

- scope clarity
- quote comparison
- budget drift
- missing decision records
- project confusion before work starts or while it is moving

### Secondary reader next

Solo GCs, renovation consultants, and small design-build operators.

They usually care about:

- cleaner project control
- better client communication
- stronger decision history
- less scattered project records
- discipline without heavyweight contractor software

## Content goals

1. Make the control problem easy to recognize.
2. Build trust through practical insight.
3. Support discovery calls and service conversations.
4. Give the founder reusable language for outreach and demos.
5. Create a small body of content that compounds.

## Content pillars

### 1. Scope clarity

Theme:

How renovation projects go wrong when the scope is weak.

Topics should cover:

- missing scope items
- unclear project intent
- the cost of vague planning
- how to move from rough idea to structured scope

### 2. Quote comparison

Theme:

How homeowners and pros compare quotes badly when there is no clear project record.

Topics should cover:

- mismatched quote assumptions
- missing items in bids
- comparing price without comparing scope
- how a cleaner record improves decisions

### 3. Budget and drift

Theme:

Why budget problems often start before the job is under control.

Topics should cover:

- weak budget framing
- hidden cost from confusion
- budget surprises caused by unclear decisions
- the relationship between scope and spend

### 4. Decisions and changes

Theme:

Why renovation decisions get lost and why change control matters.

Topics should cover:

- forgotten approvals
- revisited choices
- project confusion caused by undocumented changes
- cleaner decision history

### 5. Project record discipline

Theme:

Why scattered records create expensive confusion.

Topics should cover:

- notes in too many places
- text and email drift
- documents and photos with no structure
- one shared project record

## Post formats

Use a small set of repeatable formats.

### Format 1, founder insight post

Use for:

- one sharp idea
- one repeated pain pattern
- one observation from calls or projects

Best for:

- LinkedIn
- X threads if useful
- short founder updates

### Format 2, practical article

Use for:

- deeper explanation of one control problem
- walkthrough of how projects go wrong
- simple frameworks and checklists

Best for:

- blog posts
- landing page support content
- follow-up links after calls

### Format 3, teardown post

Use for:

- analyzing a common renovation mistake
- showing what a weak workflow looks like
- showing what a stronger workflow looks like

Best for:

- social posts
- blog posts
- demo support material

### Format 4, customer-language post

Use for:

- phrases and objections heard in interviews
- direct patterns from real conversations

Best for:

- message testing
- homepage refinement
- outreach snippets

## Reuse plan

Use one core idea multiple ways.

Example flow:

1. One customer pattern from calls
2. One short founder post about it
3. One practical article expanding it
4. One demo or discovery call example using the same language
5. One outreach line using the same pain signal

This keeps content tied to sales and validation work.

## Publishing cadence

Start small.

### Minimum useful cadence

- 1 practical article every 2 weeks
- 2 short founder posts per week
- 1 teardown or insight post per week

### Better cadence once stable

- 1 practical article per week
- 2 to 3 short founder posts per week
- 1 call insight recap per week

## Distribution

Use channels that match founder-led selling.

Start with:

- LinkedIn
- personal network
- direct follow-up after calls
- waitlist and outreach replies
- future blog or site content library

Do not optimize for broad social reach yet.

## Content quality rules

1. Focus on renovation planning pain, not broad home tips.
2. Use plain language.
3. Stay practical.
4. Prefer concrete examples over generic advice.
5. Keep every post tied to a real workflow problem.
6. Do not lead with AI.
7. Do not sound like generic contractor software marketing.

## Good content signals

A strong piece of content should do at least one of these:

- make a buyer say, that is exactly my problem
- help a buyer describe their pain more clearly
- give the founder stronger language for calls
- create a useful follow-up asset after discovery
- support a service or pilot conversation

## Weak content signals

Avoid content that:

- chases generic renovation inspiration
- gives broad decorating tips
- sounds like SEO filler
- explains features without pain context
- attracts readers who will never buy or test

## Operating workflow

Use this simple loop:

1. pull a repeated pain pattern from calls
2. turn it into a short post
3. expand the strongest ones into articles
4. reuse the best lines in outreach and demos
5. log which topics create replies, calls, or stronger resonance

## Success measures

Track:

- replies to content
- direct messages from relevant buyers
- content links used in follow-up
- calls influenced by content
- repeated pain patterns strengthened by content

## Recommendation

Keep the engine narrow.

Start with five pillars, three formats, and a small publishing cadence.

The goal is not volume.
The goal is trust and sales support.
