# Boilerhaus Ontario Incorporation Guide v1

## Purpose

Create a practical checklist and setup guide for incorporating Boilerhaus in Ontario, with ScopeHouse run as a product offering or operating name under Boilerhaus.

This is a founder guide, not a substitute for legal or tax advice.

## Recommended setup

Use an Ontario business corporation as the legal entity.

Recommended structure:

- legal entity: Boilerhaus Inc. or Boilerhaus Technologies Inc.
- product or operating name: ScopeHouse

Why this setup:

- Ontario lets you incorporate a business corporation online through the Ontario Business Registry.
- If you want a named corporation, Ontario requires an Ontario-biased or weighted Nuans report unless you choose a numbered corporation.
- If the corporation carries on business or identifies itself to the public under a name other than its corporate name, that name must be registered under the Business Names Act.

That means the clean path is:

1. incorporate the company as Boilerhaus Inc. if the name is available
2. register ScopeHouse as the business name if you will market or contract under ScopeHouse

## Fast decision

### Option A, recommended

- incorporate: Boilerhaus Inc.
- register business name: ScopeHouse

Best if you want the company brand and the product brand kept separate.

### Option B, fastest filing

- incorporate a numbered Ontario corporation
- later file Articles of Amendment to change the legal name to Boilerhaus Inc. or another final name
- register ScopeHouse as a business name if needed

Best if speed matters more than branding on day one.

## What Ontario requires and what to expect

### Incorporation route

Ontario lets you incorporate a business corporation online through the Ontario Business Registry. Online incorporation is listed as immediate and costs $300.

### Named versus numbered corporation

Ontario says you can either propose a name for the corporation or request a number name. If you register as a business corporation and you are not using a number name, Ontario requires an Ontario-biased or weighted Nuans report.

### Business name for ScopeHouse

Under Ontario’s Business Names Act, a corporation cannot carry on business or identify itself to the public under a name other than its corporate name unless that name is registered. If Boilerhaus is the legal company and ScopeHouse is the public-facing offering, register ScopeHouse as the business name.

### Ontario account access and company key

Ontario now uses My Ontario Account and Ontario Business Account for the Ontario Business Registry. To manage an existing business profile, filings, and returns, you need the linked account setup, and a company key is used to link and manage the business profile.

### Initial Return and updates

Ontario corporations must file an Initial Return within 60 days after incorporation. If information changes later, Ontario says you must update the ministry within 15 days using the appropriate filing.

### Annual Return

Ontario corporations can file the annual return directly in the Ontario Business Registry. Ontario lists the annual return fee for an Ontario corporation as $0 when filed online.

### CRA business number and corporation income tax account

The CRA says that when you incorporate in Ontario, the corporation is automatically assigned a business number and a corporation income tax program account. The CRA also says you must add the business number to your CRA account to manage it online.

### GST HST

The CRA says most businesses must register for GST HST once they are no longer small suppliers. For most businesses, the small supplier threshold is $30,000 over four consecutive calendar quarters, with different timing rules if you exceed it in a single quarter. The CRA also allows voluntary registration if you make taxable sales in Canada.

### Payroll

The CRA says you may need a payroll account if you are an employer, and it generally considers you an employer if you pay salaries, wages, bonuses, vacation pay, or tips to employees.

### Beneficial ownership register

Ontario requires privately held Ontario business corporations to keep beneficial ownership or individuals with significant control information on file. Ontario says this information must be updated at least once a year and within 15 days of the corporation becoming aware of a relevant change.
