# ScopeHouse Weekly Business Metrics Review v1

## Purpose

Define the weekly business scorecard for ScopeHouse.

This review exists to answer one question:

Are we learning fast enough about demand, fit, conversion, and early revenue?

## Rules

1. Track metrics that change decisions.
2. Avoid vanity metrics.
3. Review the same set weekly.
4. Tie each metric to an action.
5. Separate leading signals from lagging results.

## Review cadence

Run this once per week.

Suggested structure:

- 15 minutes, pipeline review
- 15 minutes, validation and interview review
- 15 minutes, revenue and pilot review
- 15 minutes, product-linked usage review
- 15 minutes, decisions and next actions

## Weekly scorecard

### 1. Pipeline metrics

Track:

- new leads added
- total active leads
- outreach sent
- replies received
- discovery calls booked
- discovery calls completed
- demos completed
- leads moved to design-partner-priority

Why these matter:

They show whether the top of the funnel is producing enough qualified conversations.

Questions to ask:

- Are enough new leads entering the pipeline?
- Is outreach turning into real conversations?
- Are calls turning into qualified next steps?

## 2. Interview and validation metrics

Track:

- customer interviews completed
- homeowners interviewed
- pros interviewed
- strong pain signals found
- repeated objection patterns
- repeated workflow pain patterns
- design partner candidates identified

Why these matter:

They show whether you are learning enough about real problems and segment fit.

Questions to ask:

- Which pain points repeat most?
- Which segment shows stronger urgency?
- Which objections are increasing?

## 3. Revenue metrics

Track:

- founder-led service conversations
- service proposals sent
- service deals closed
- service revenue booked
- paid pilot conversations
- paid pilots closed
- MRR committed if any

Why these matter:

They show whether the business is moving from interest to money.

Questions to ask:

- Is revenue coming from service first, pilot first, or neither?
- Where is pricing resistance showing up?
- Which offer closes fastest?

## 4. Activation metrics

Track:

- active beta users
- new projects created
- intake completed
- scope draft completed
- export generated
- first-week active users

Why these matter:

They show whether the product creates enough value after first contact.

Questions to ask:

- Are users reaching the core planning loop?
- Where are users dropping off?
- Which setup step needs more support?

## 5. Retention and engagement metrics

Track:

- users returning after first session
- active design partners this week
- follow-up sessions completed
- feedback sessions completed
- users stuck with no next step

Why these matter:

They show whether early users are staying engaged long enough to teach the product.

Questions to ask:

- Are early users coming back?
- Are we keeping design partners active?
- Where is follow-through weak?

## 6. Conversion metrics

Track:

- lead to discovery call rate
- discovery call to demo rate
- discovery call to service conversation rate
- discovery call to design partner rate
- service conversation to close rate
- pilot conversation to close rate

Why these matter:

They show where the commercial motion is breaking.

Questions to ask:

- Where do leads stall?
- Which route converts best?
- Are weak-fit leads consuming too much time?

## 7. Operating discipline metrics

Track:

- active leads with next action set
- overdue follow-ups
- interviews logged within 24 hours
- tracker rows updated this week
- open objections added to objection sheet

Why these matter:

They show whether the founder workflow is tight enough to trust the rest of the numbers.

Questions to ask:

- Are we letting leads go stale?
- Is the tracker clean enough to operate from?
- Are we capturing learning fast enough?

## Decision rules

### If pipeline is weak

Increase outreach or referral activity.

### If interviews are strong but revenue is weak

Review pricing, offer clarity, and trust objections.

### If service closes but product use is weak

Improve onboarding and core activation.

### If design partners are identified but go inactive

Tighten follow-up and reduce onboarding friction.

### If one segment keeps outperforming

Bias the next week toward that segment.

## Reminder

The weekly review should lead to decisions.

A good scorecard helps answer:

- who to talk to next
- what to fix next
- what to sell next
- what to stop doing
