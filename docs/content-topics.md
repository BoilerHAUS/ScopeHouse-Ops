# ScopeHouse First 10 Content Topics v1

## Purpose

Create the first batch of practical content topics for ScopeHouse.

These topics should:

- support founder sales conversations
- sharpen the control problem
- attract the right kind of buyer
- build trust through practical insight

## Topic list

### 1. Why renovation quotes are hard to compare when the scope is weak

Angle:

Most people think they are comparing prices, but they are often comparing different assumptions, missing items, and unclear scope.

### 2. The real cost of vague renovation planning

Angle:

A project does not need to be underway before weak planning starts costing money. The damage often starts when the record is still loose.

### 3. How homeowners lose control of a renovation before construction even starts

Angle:

The project begins to drift when decisions, notes, quotes, and expectations are spread across too many places.

### 4. What a clean renovation scope should do before you ask for quotes

Angle:

A good scope is not a perfect technical document. It is a clear working record that makes quote comparison and decision-making harder to distort.

### 5. Budget drift usually starts with unclear decisions

Angle:

Budgets do not only fail because prices rise. They fail because the project record does not hold onto decisions clearly enough.

### 6. Why renovation changes feel chaotic without a decision trail

Angle:

Changes are not the real problem. Missing records of what changed, why, and who approved it is the real problem.

### 7. The hidden risk of running a renovation out of notes, texts, and spreadsheets

Angle:

Most projects look manageable in pieces, until the record has to hold real scope, quote, decision, and change pressure.

### 8. What small renovation pros need before they need contractor ERP software

Angle:

Many small pros do not need a heavy system. They need a cleaner control layer for client projects.

### 9. How to compare renovation quotes against project intent, not memory

Angle:

The buyer should not have to remember every scope detail while reviewing bids. The project record should do that job.

### 10. One shared project record is more valuable than another renovation app

Angle:

The value is not more software. The value is one place where scope, budget, quotes, decisions, and changes stop drifting apart.
